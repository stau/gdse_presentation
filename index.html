Globally distributed software engineering

Challenges & issues

  • Geographical dispersion
  • Cultural differences
  • Timezone differences
  • Loss of "teamness"
  • Coordination breakdown
  • Loss of communication richness

SCRUM

In a globally distributed setting

SCRUM

How does it work?

  • Sprint meetings
  • Sprint planning
  • Daily progress (stand-up) meetings
  • Sprint & product backlog

SCRUM

Sprint meetings

  • Requirements discussion
  • Features to be implemented that sprint

SCRUM

Sprint meetings: Challenges

  • Geographic dispersal of development team
  • Differing cultural backgrounds

SCRUM

Sprint meetings: Solutions

  • Provide always-on videochat capabilities
  • Organize team outings outside of crunch-time

SCRUM

Sprint planning

  • Held at start of each sprint
  • Discuss which features to implement that sprint

SCRUM

Sprint planning: Challenges

  • Difficult to do without management software supporting geographical dispersion
  • Similar to those associated with Sprint meetings

SCRUM

Sprint planning: Solutions

  • Use collaborative backlog management software (e.g. tinyPM)
  • Similar challenges, similar solutions

SCRUM

Daily progress (stand-up) meetings

  • Held at the start of each day
  • Short
  • Discussion of progress made since previous day
  • Discuss impediments to progress
  • Discuss what to do that day

SCRUM

Daily progress (stand-up) meetings: Challenges

  • Stakeholders not necessarily at same geographical location
  • Time zone differences make holding meetings more difficult

SCRUM

Daily progress (stand-up) meetings: Solutions

  • Utilize (permanent) videochat to lower the bar for team member sharing
  • Plan meetings carefully w.r.t. any overlapping work hours
  • Ask team members to attend meetings outside of work hours

SCRUM

Product backlog

  • List of requirements for entire product
  • Presented in use cases: per functionality, tasks that need to be done

SCRUM

Product backlog: Challenges

  • Keep the product & sprint backlogs synchronized across team distribution sites
  • Product owners may not be available all the time

SCRUM

Product backlog: Solutions

  • Collaborative backlog management software for planning and synchronizing
  • Clear communication to stakeholders that they need to attend meetings

Conclusion

  • SCRUM can help in migrating to a distributed setting
  • High-tech solutions can help mitigate communication issues
  • Not a silver bullet: strong planning still needed
